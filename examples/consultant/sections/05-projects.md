# Projects

## Tookland Midsummer Festival Expansion

Transformed the annual Tookland festival from a local gathering into the Shire's premier summer event.
**Technologies:** Event planning, vendor management, marketing, entertainment booking

- Grew attendance from 150 to 400+ guests over 5 years through targeted outreach
- Negotiated Gandalf the Grey as headline fireworks supplier, creating signature event attraction
- Established rotating venue system across 3 Tookland locations to reduce site fatigue

## Took–Brandybuck Youth Exchange Programme

Inter-family cultural exchange sending young hobbits between Great Smials and Brandy Hall for seasonal apprenticeships.
**Technologies:** Programme design, family liaison, participant matching, logistics

- Designed 3-month exchange curriculum covering estate management, cookery, and regional lore
- Successfully placed 12 participants in first two cohorts with 100% completion rate
- Strengthened Took–Brandybuck alliance and improved cross-regional understanding

## Shire Rumour Network Mapping

Informal study documenting the speed and reliability of information flow through Shire social networks.
**Technologies:** Social observation, route analysis, timing studies

- Tracked the propagation of 20 notable rumours from origin to full Shire saturation
- Identified the Green Dragon Inn (Bywater) as the primary information hub of the Westfarthing
- Determined average rumour propagation speed: 2 days to reach all four Farthings
